01 — Definition

What Are
Liminal Spaces?

Liminal spaces are transitional areas — hallways, waiting rooms, empty parking lots at 3 AM. Places designed to be passed through, never lingered in. They exist as thresholds between one destination and the next.

When captured empty, stripped of their intended purpose and the people who give them meaning, they become deeply unsettling. Something about them triggers a primal recognition — a memory of a place you've been, a dream you can't quite recall.

The word liminal comes from the Latin līmen, meaning "threshold."

03 — Psychology

Why Do They
Unsettle Us?

The unease isn't random. It's a collision between what your brain expects and what it finds.

01

Spatial Memory

Your brain maps spaces by their purpose and occupants. A hallway without walkers, a mall without shoppers — the map breaks. You recognize the place but not the context.

02

Absence of Purpose

These spaces were designed for human activity. Without it, they become architectural ghosts — structures that have outlived their meaning.

03

Temporal Displacement

Empty spaces exist outside of time. There are no clocks, no schedules, no day or night. You could have been here for five minutes or five hours.

04

The Backrooms

Internet folklore imagines an infinite network of empty rooms behind reality — yellowish wallpaper, damp carpet, buzzing fluorescents. A place you "noclip" into. The fear that emptiness might be endless.

04 — The Experience

What It Feels Like

You walk into a building you don't recognize.

The lights are on. The air conditioning hums.

You passed through the entrance, but you don't remember opening a door.

The hallway stretches in both directions. Identical.

Your footsteps echo, but just a half-second too late.

There's a chair by the wall. Someone was sitting here recently.

A clock on the wall. It says 3:14. It always says 3:14.

You hear the soft buzz of a vending machine around the corner.

When you turn the corner, there's another hallway.

You've been here before.

You've always been here.
